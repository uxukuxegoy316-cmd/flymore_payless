Orlando is the most visited destination in the United States, welcoming over 75 million visitors annually. With Walt Disney World, Universal Studios, SeaWorld, and dozens of other attractions, the city draws families from across the country year-round. This massive demand makes finding cheap flights important — and possible, if you know the right strategies.

Orlando has two airports: Orlando International (MCO) and Orlando Sanford International (SFO). MCO is larger and closer to Disney World and Universal, served by all major airlines. Sanford is smaller, served mainly by discount carriers, and sits about 35 miles further from the theme parks. Always compare total costs including ground transportation.

The cheapest months to fly to Orlando are September and early October, when Florida summer heat has faded but theme park crowds are lower. January and February (excluding school holidays) are also affordable. Avoid spring break (March-April), summer (June-August), and the week between Christmas and New Year's.
