Nashville has transformed from a country music niche destination into one of America's most vibrant, diverse, and sought-after cities. The honky-tonks on Broadway are iconic, but Nashville's restaurant scene (James Beard-winning chefs), art scene, and genuine neighborly warmth attract visitors who've never owned a pair of cowboy boots.

Nashville International Airport (BNA) has expanded significantly to meet booming demand. Southwest Airlines has strong Nashville presence. American, Delta, United, Spirit, and Frontier all serve BNA. The competition has helped keep Nashville flight prices reasonable despite the city's soaring popularity.

Spring (March-May) brings pleasant weather and the CMA Fest (June) draws enormous crowds and prices. October brings fall colors and moderate prices. New Year's Eve in Nashville is becoming increasingly popular. The cheapest months are January, February, and the quieter stretches of summer when heat reduces some visitor demand.

Nashville is within driving distance of genuinely spectacular nature — the Great Smoky Mountains are 4 hours east, Mammoth Cave National Park 2 hours north, and Jack Daniel's Distillery is an easy day trip. Flying into Nashville opens up the entire Mid-South region for exploration.
