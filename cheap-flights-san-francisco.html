The San Francisco Bay Area is served by three airports — San Francisco International (SFO), Oakland International (OAK), and San Jose International (SJC) — creating more options for budget travelers than almost any other major US metropolitan area. Understanding which airport best serves your Bay Area destination is crucial.

SFO is the largest with the most international flights and United Airlines' main West Coast hub. It's located 14 miles south of downtown, with BART rail connecting in about 30 minutes for $10. OAK is across the Bay and often has cheaper fares — it's a Southwest hub and serves many budget carriers. SJC serves Silicon Valley directly and is closest to tech hubs like Mountain View, Cupertino, and Santa Clara.

United has a massive hub at SFO providing extensive domestic and international coverage. Southwest dominates OAK. Delta, American, Alaska, and international carriers serve SFO extensively. The competition across three airports keeps Bay Area pricing competitive.

San Francisco's famous fog (Karl the Fog) affects summer mornings but clears by afternoon. Paradoxically, September and October — when the fog clears and sunny weather arrives — are SF's best months but also more expensive for travel. December through February is cheapest but coldest and wettest.
