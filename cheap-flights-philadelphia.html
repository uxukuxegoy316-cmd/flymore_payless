Philadelphia is perhaps America's most underrated major city — extraordinary history (Independence Hall, Liberty Bell), world-class museums (Philadelphia Museum of Art, Franklin Institute), exceptional dining, and passionate sports culture, all at prices well below comparable East Coast cities like New York or Boston.

Philadelphia International Airport (PHL) is American Airlines' third-largest hub after DFW and MIA, providing excellent connectivity from across the US and internationally. The airport is located just 7 miles from Center City, with SEPTA rail connecting airport to downtown in about 25 minutes for $7.

For travelers targeting New York City, Philadelphia is worth considering as an alternative entry point. Philadelphia flights are often $50-150 cheaper than equivalent New York flights, and Amtrak's Acela and Northeast Regional trains reach Penn Station Manhattan in 65-90 minutes for $40-120. The math often favors flying to Philadelphia.

The cheapest months to visit Philadelphia are January, February, and November. Independence Day week (July 4) is expensive but spectacular for history lovers. Summer is busy with tourists. Philadelphia's colonial history sites are excellent year-round.
