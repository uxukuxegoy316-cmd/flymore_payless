Travel insurance is one of the most misunderstood aspects of air travel planning. Many travelers skip it entirely, assuming they'll be fine. Others buy expensive policies that duplicate coverage they already have. The reality is nuanced — sometimes travel insurance is essential, sometimes it's unnecessary.

Trip cancellation coverage is the most valuable component for most travelers. If you're booking non-refundable international flights months in advance, trip cancellation insurance protects against unexpected events like illness, family emergencies, or job loss. For refundable domestic flights, this coverage is less necessary.

Medical coverage while traveling is critical for international trips. US health insurance typically provides limited or no coverage outside the country. Travel insurance fills this gap, covering emergency medical treatment, hospital stays, and medical evacuation — which can cost tens of thousands of dollars without insurance.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is travel insurance worth buying for domestic flights?
For domestic flights, the need is lower than international. If you're booking refundable tickets, coverage may be unnecessary. If you have non-refundable bookings for important travel, a basic policy can provide peace of mind. Call (866) 228-4952 for advice on your specific situation.
What does travel insurance NOT cover?
Standard policies don't cover pre-existing medical conditions (without a waiver), pandemics (check your specific policy), losses due to intoxication, self-inflicted injuries, or travel to countries under government travel advisories.
Can I buy travel insurance after booking my flight?
Yes, you can buy travel insurance after booking. However, buy it soon after booking to maximize coverage for pre-departure events. The earlier you buy, the more pre-trip events you're covered for.
