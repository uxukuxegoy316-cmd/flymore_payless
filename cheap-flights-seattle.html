Seattle has transformed from a regional Pacific Northwest city into one of America's most vibrant and internationally significant urban destinations. Home to Amazon, Microsoft, Boeing, and a world-class tech scene, Seattle attracts both business and leisure travelers year-round. The coffee culture, Pike Place Market, and Space Needle are iconic, but the real draws are the surrounding natural landscape — mountains, islands, and rainforests within an hour of downtown.

Alaska Airlines calls Seattle home, with their main hub at Sea-Tac providing comprehensive domestic coverage. Delta has a significant international hub operation here, with excellent trans-Pacific connections. Southwest, United, and American all serve Seattle. The combination creates strong competition that generally keeps prices reasonable.

The Pacific Northwest's famous rain makes pricing interesting. The 'rainy season' (October-May) sees lower flight prices as some tourists are deterred by gray weather. But Seattle summers (June-September) are genuinely spectacular — sunny, mild, and beautiful — with corresponding higher prices. If you're flexible on weather, the off-season deals are significant.

Seattle is the primary departure point for Alaska cruises, making it a major cruise embarkation port. If you're planning an Alaska cruise, pairing your flight with cruise booking through our agents at (866) 228-4952 can sometimes yield package benefits.
