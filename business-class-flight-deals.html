The reason is consolidator fares — bulk rates that airlines sell to travel agencies that are never published publicly. These fares can represent significant savings on transatlantic and transpacific business class routes, where the difference between published and agent-exclusive pricing can be hundreds or even thousands of dollars per ticket.
