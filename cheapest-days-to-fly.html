The day you fly makes a bigger difference to your ticket price than most people realize. Tuesday and Wednesday flights are consistently 15-30% cheaper than peak travel days. Airlines fill their planes with business travelers Monday through Thursday, but leisure travelers on weekends drive up Friday and Sunday prices dramatically.

Saturday morning flights are a well-kept secret. Most leisure travelers want to arrive Friday night or Saturday evening, making Saturday morning departures surprisingly affordable. If you can handle an early start, this trick saves real money.

The absolute worst days to fly price-wise are Friday afternoons and Sunday evenings. Everyone is traveling for work on Fridays and returning home on Sundays, creating massive demand that airlines exploit with higher prices.
