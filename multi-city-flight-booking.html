Multi-city flight itineraries — booking flights into one destination and out of another — open up entirely new ways to travel that aren't possible with simple round trips. Done right, they can be cheaper than standard round trips while dramatically expanding your travel experience.

An open jaw ticket lets you fly into one city and out of another, allowing you to travel between them overland. For example, fly into London and out of Rome after spending two weeks traveling through Europe. Compared to booking London round trip and buying a separate internal European flight home, this can save $200-400.

Stopovers — spending more than 24 hours in a connecting city — are one of travel's best-kept secrets. Many airlines allow free extended stopovers on certain itineraries. Fly New York to Tokyo on Japan Airlines with a free stopover in Tokyo on the way to Bangkok, essentially adding Japan to your itinerary for no extra flight cost.
