Dallas is served by two major airports — Dallas/Fort Worth International (DFW) and Dallas Love Field (DAL) — creating useful options for budget-conscious travelers. DFW is American Airlines' main hub and one of the busiest airports in the world. Love Field is Southwest's most important historical base.

DFW's size brings both advantages (more flights, more competition, more connections) and disadvantages (confusing navigation, longer taxi times, further from downtown Dallas). Love Field is much closer to downtown Dallas and has a significantly more pleasant passenger experience — but serves fewer cities and airlines.

American Airlines dominates DFW with an enormous hub operation. This means excellent connectivity to virtually every US city, but also means American prices at DFW face less competition from other carriers on many routes. Southwest's Love Field operation provides a genuine alternative for cities served by both.

Dallas has surprisingly low visitor appeal awareness given its actual offering — world-class museums including the incredible Perot Museum, excellent dining, the Sixth Floor Museum at Dealey Plaza, and as a gateway to the Texas Hill Country and beyond. Flights to Dallas are generally well-priced due to the competition between DFW and Love Field.

Should I fly into DFW or Love Field?

What is the cheapest time to fly to Dallas?
January, February, and September tend to have the lowest prices. Summer can be surprisingly affordable despite heat. Major events like the State Fair of Texas (October) spike hotel prices more than flights. Call (866) 228-4952 for current Dallas fare comparisons.
Is Dallas a good city to visit?
Yes — Dallas is significantly underrated as a tourist destination. World-class arts (Dallas Museum of Art, Nasher Sculpture Center), excellent dining, the Perot Museum of Nature and Science, and easy day trips to Fort Worth's Stockyards make it a compelling destination.
