The Los Angeles to Las Vegas route is one of the most traveled corridors in the United States, with thousands of people making the trip weekly by both air and car. The 270-mile drive takes 4-5 hours in normal traffic (often much longer with LA traffic), while the flight is just 55 minutes. The question of drive versus fly depends on price, timing, and your specific situation.

The I-15 highway between LA and Vegas is notoriously congested on Friday afternoons and Sunday evenings. Holiday weekends can see 6-8 hour drive times. For these peak departure times, flying makes overwhelming sense — and flight prices from LA to Vegas are surprisingly affordable, sometimes as low as $39-79 each way.

Multiple airlines serve the LA-Vegas route. Southwest operates from Burbank (BUR), Long Beach (LGB), and LAX. Spirit, Frontier, American, Delta, and Alaska all serve LAX to LAS. The extraordinary competition keeps prices low year-round.

For travelers departing from the San Fernando Valley, Burbank to Las Vegas with Southwest is often the best option — Southwest's free bags, combined with the smaller, faster airport experience at BUR, creates superior total value.
