Airports are designed to separate travelers from their money. From $18 cocktails to $40 daily parking, the costs add up dramatically. Knowing where to save and where to spend is essential for the budget-conscious traveler.

Airport parking is one of the biggest unnecessary expenses. Official airport parking at major airports can cost $20-50 per day. Third-party parking services (SpotHero, ParkWhiz) offer spaces near the airport for 30-60% less. Rideshare services (Uber, Lyft) are often cheapest for shorter trips.

Airport lounges provide free food, drinks, fast WiFi, and comfortable seating — but access typically requires business class tickets or certain credit cards. The American Express Platinum card, Chase Sapphire Reserve, and similar premium travel cards often provide lounge access that more than pays for the annual fee in airport comfort and savings.

TSA PreCheck ($85 for 5 years) and Global Entry ($100 for 5 years, includes PreCheck) dramatically reduce security wait times. For frequent travelers — even 4-5 trips per year — these programs are worth every penny. Many credit cards even reimburse the application fee.

Is TSA PreCheck worth the money?
For anyone flying 3 or more times per year, absolutely yes. TSA PreCheck costs $17 per year and saves 20-40 minutes per trip at security. Many travel credit cards reimburse the fee entirely. Call (866) 228-4952 and we can advise on the best approach for your travel frequency.
How can I get free airport lounge access?
Premium travel credit cards (Amex Platinum, Chase Sapphire Reserve) offer Priority Pass membership providing access to 1,300+ airport lounges worldwide. Business class tickets also typically include lounge access. Ask our agents at (866) 228-4952 about booking premium cabin fares.
What is the cheapest way to get to the airport?
Public transit is cheapest where available. Rideshare (Uber/Lyft) is typically cheaper than taxis for airport runs. Third-party parking apps beat airport rates. For the airport itself, arriving early with packed snacks eliminates expensive airport food purchases.
