Boston is one of America's most beloved cities — world-class universities, revolutionary history, outstanding seafood, and a passionate sports culture. Logan International Airport is conveniently located just 3 miles from downtown Boston, and the Silver Line bus connects airport to South Station for just $2.40.

The cheapest months to fly to Boston are January, February, and the quieter weeks of spring before graduation season. October is famous for New England fall foliage — stunning to see but prices are higher. Summer (June-August) sees significant tourist demand, especially for graduation season (May-June) when every New England university holds ceremonies.

JetBlue operates one of its largest hubs at Boston Logan, providing excellent service to Florida, the Caribbean, and transcontinental routes. Delta, American, and United all have significant Boston presence. Southwest serves Boston with their typically competitive fares.

For travelers coming from Europe, Boston is a great alternative gateway to the East Coast compared to New York — often cheaper transatlantic flights and less congestion.
