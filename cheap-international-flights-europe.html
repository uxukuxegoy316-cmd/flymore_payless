Your US departure city significantly impacts your transatlantic fare. New York, Boston, and Miami have the most European flight options and most competitive pricing. From the Midwest and Southeast, connecting through East Coast hubs is common and often economical. From the West Coast, flights are longer but often competitively priced to both European and Middle Eastern hubs.

European entry points vary dramatically in price. London, Dublin, Lisbon, and Reykjavik tend to have the lowest transatlantic fares due to the number of airlines serving these routes. Paris, Amsterdam, and Frankfurt are next. Mediterranean destinations like Rome, Athens, and Barcelona are often best reached via a connection at a cheaper European gateway.

Shoulder season — May/June and September/October — is Europe's secret gift to budget travelers. Crowds are lighter than peak summer, weather is often excellent, prices are significantly lower, and many European cities are at their most beautiful.
