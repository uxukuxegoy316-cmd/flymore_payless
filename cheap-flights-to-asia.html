Transpacific travel has rebounded strongly and routes between the US and Asia are among the most sought-after long-haul itineraries for American travelers. Japan in particular has seen extraordinary demand following its post-pandemic reopening, with Tokyo and Osaka consistently ranking among the most desired destinations.

West Coast US cities — Los Angeles, San Francisco, Seattle — are the primary transpacific gateways with the most direct flights and lowest fares to Asian destinations. From the East Coast, connecting through West Coast hubs or through Middle Eastern hubs (Dubai, Doha) on Emirates or Qatar can yield competitive pricing.

Japan remains America's most popular Asian destination. Japanese hospitality, food culture, ancient temples, modern technology, and natural beauty create an incomparable travel experience. Tokyo and Osaka are the main entry points, with domestic travel between them and other Japanese cities easy via the Shinkansen bullet train.
