New York City is served by three major airports — JFK, LaGuardia (LGA), and Newark (EWR) — giving travelers more options and more chances to find a cheap flight than almost anywhere else in the world. Knowing which airport to use for your specific situation can save you significant money.

JFK is the largest and most connected, handling the most international flights and serving airlines like Delta, American, JetBlue, and numerous international carriers. It's located in Queens and transportation to Manhattan takes 45-75 minutes. LaGuardia is closest to Midtown Manhattan (30-45 minutes) and serves mostly domestic routes. Newark, technically in New Jersey, is 45-60 minutes from Midtown but often has the cheapest fares, especially on United's hub routes.

The cheapest months to visit New York are January, February, and November (excluding holidays). Summer (June-August) is expensive due to tourism demand. The week between Christmas and New Year's is peak pricing season.
