Choosing the right airline can save you money and make your journey significantly more pleasant. US airlines have distinct personalities, strengths, and weaknesses that make different carriers better for different travelers and routes.

American Airlines operates the largest route network in the US and has strong presence at Dallas-Fort Worth, Miami, and Charlotte hubs. Delta consistently ranks highest for on-time performance and customer satisfaction among legacy carriers, with strong hubs in Atlanta, Minneapolis, and Detroit. United offers the most extensive international network, particularly for trans-Pacific routes.

Southwest is unlike any other US airline — open seating, two free checked bags, no change fees, and a loyal following. For domestic travel, Southwest's total value often beats budget carriers when bags are factored in. JetBlue offers the best domestic legroom and a genuinely premium economy product (Mint) for transcontinental routes.

Spirit and Frontier compete on the lowest base fares but charge for everything else. They're genuinely competitive for travelers who pack light, book early, and are flexible. For others, the hidden fees make them more expensive than mainstream carriers.
