Las Vegas is one of the most competitive air travel markets in the United States, which is great news for budget-conscious travelers. With over 50 nonstop flights operating daily from major US cities, competition keeps prices lower than almost any other leisure destination.

The cheapest months to fly to Las Vegas are January and February (excluding Super Bowl weekend), September, and November (excluding Thanksgiving). Summer can be surprisingly affordable too since extreme desert heat keeps some travelers away, resulting in lower fares on certain routes.

Almost every major US city has direct service to Las Vegas McCarran International Airport (LAS). American Airlines, Delta, United, Southwest, JetBlue, Alaska, Spirit, and Frontier all serve Vegas extensively. Southwest is often the best value given their no-fee baggage policy.
