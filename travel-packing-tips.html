Baggage fees are one of the biggest unnecessary costs in air travel today. American travelers pay billions in baggage fees annually — money that could stay in their pockets with smarter packing. The solution isn't just packing less (though that helps) — it's packing smarter.

Understanding each airline's carry-on size and weight limits is essential. Most US airlines allow one carry-on bag and one personal item in economy. Carry-on dimensions are typically 22 x 14 x 9 inches or similar. Personal items (under-seat bags) have smaller limits. Budget carriers Spirit and Frontier charge even for carry-ons above their personal item size — knowing the rules before you pack saves real money.

The capsule wardrobe concept revolutionizes travel packing. Instead of packing complete outfits for each day, pack a smaller number of versatile pieces that combine into multiple outfits. Dark colors that hide wrinkles and don't show dirt, quick-drying fabrics, and items that can dress up or down with accessories form the foundation.

Can I bring a backpack as a carry-on?
Most airlines allow a backpack as a carry-on if it fits the size requirements (typically 22 x 14 x 9 inches). Budget carriers (Spirit, Frontier) may charge for larger backpacks. Call (866) 228-4952 for your specific airline's carry-on policies before packing.
What is the lightest effective travel luggage?
Osprey and Eagle Creek make excellent lightweight carry-on bags. For checked luggage, Away and Rimowa are popular with good warranty. For ultra-light travel, a quality 40-liter backpack (Osprey Farpoint, REI Co-op Ruckpack) avoids airline sizing concerns entirely.
