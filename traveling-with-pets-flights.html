Flying with pets requires careful advance planning — each airline has different policies, fees, and restrictions, and getting the details wrong can result in your pet being denied at the airport. Understanding the rules and booking correctly from the start is essential.

Small pets (cats and small dogs) can typically fly in the cabin if they fit in an approved soft-sided carrier under the seat in front of you. The combined weight of pet plus carrier typically must be under 20 pounds. Cabin pet fees range from $95-125 each way depending on the airline. Spots for cabin pets are limited — book early.

Larger dogs and breeds that can't fit under the seat must fly as checked baggage or air cargo. This distinction matters enormously — checked baggage is handled differently from cargo, and some airlines have eliminated pet checked baggage programs entirely. Our agents at (866) 228-4952 know each airline's current pet programs.

Brachycephalic breeds (bulldogs, pugs, boxers, Persian cats) are restricted from flying as cargo by most airlines due to breathing risks at altitude and in cargo holds. Some airlines restrict these breeds from cabin travel too. If you have a flat-faced breed, call (866) 228-4952 before making any travel plans to identify which airlines and options are available.

Which airline is best for flying with pets?
Alaska Airlines and Southwest are generally considered pet-friendly with clear policies. American, Delta, and United also have established pet programs. Call (866) 228-4952 and our agents will confirm current pet policies and availability for your specific route and pet.
Are there health certificate requirements for pet travel?
Many airlines require a USDA-accredited veterinarian health certificate issued within 10 days of travel for domestic flights. International pet travel requires additional documentation. Call (866) 228-4952 for specific requirements for your route and airline.
