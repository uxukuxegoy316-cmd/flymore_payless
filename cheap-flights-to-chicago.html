Chicago is served by two major airports — O'Hare International (ORD) and Midway International (MDW) — creating excellent competition that benefits budget-conscious travelers. O'Hare is one of the busiest airports in the world and serves all major carriers. Midway is Southwest's largest hub and consistently offers competitive fares.

For travelers on the Southwest and Spirit routes, Midway often wins on price. For flights on American, United, Delta, and international connections, O'Hare is typically your only option. Both airports are served by the CTA Blue Line (O'Hare) and Orange Line (Midway) making them both accessible from downtown Chicago for about $3.

The cheapest time to fly to Chicago is winter — January and February — when temperatures drop but prices follow. If you're brave enough to handle Midwest winters, you'll find excellent flight deals. Fall (September-October) is another sweet spot with comfortable weather and lower prices.

Summer in Chicago is beautiful and the city hosts world-class events — but prices reflect that. The Lollapalooza music festival in August and the Chicago Air and Water Show drive prices sky-high during those specific weekends.
