A layover can feel like lost time or like a bonus adventure — the difference is almost entirely in your mindset and preparation. Long layovers of 4+ hours in the right airports offer genuine opportunities to explore, rest, or simply experience world-class airport facilities.

The minimum connection time varies by airport, airline, and whether you're making a domestic-to-domestic connection or an international connection requiring customs and immigration. Some airports are genuinely excellent for long layovers. Singapore's Changi Airport has a waterfall, movie theater, and outdoor garden. Doha's Hamad International has art installations and a swimming pool. Dubai International has extensive shopping and dining. In the US, Dallas-Fort Worth and Denver have strong airport amenities and adjacent hotels for overnight layovers.

Airport lounges transform long layovers from endurance events into comfortable rest periods. Access through credit cards (Amex Platinum, Chase Sapphire Reserve) or business class tickets turns any 3+ hour layover into an opportunity for good food, drinks, showers, and comfortable seating. Frequently Asked Questions

How long of a layover do I need for international connections?
For international connections requiring customs and immigration, plan at least 2-3 hours minimum. If connecting from international to domestic through a US airport (clearing customs), 2.5 hours is minimum and 3+ is more comfortable. Call (866) 228-4952 — our agents always build safe connection times.
Can I leave the airport during a layover?
Yes, if your layover is long enough and you have the right visa status. For US airports, no additional visa is needed. For international airports, check whether your passport requires a transit visa for that country. Call (866) 228-4952 for advice on your specific layover destination.
What do I do if I miss a connection?
Go immediately to your airline's service desk or gate agent and call (866) 228-4952 simultaneously. Our agents often rebook faster than airport agents. If the missed connection was the airline's fault (delay, cancellation), they must rebook you at no cost on the next available flight.
