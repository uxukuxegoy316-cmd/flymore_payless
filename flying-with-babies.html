Flying with a baby feels overwhelming for first-time parent travelers, but with the right preparation and correct booking, it can go remarkably smoothly. The most important step is getting the booking details right from the start — and that's where our agents at (866) 228-4952 provide invaluable help.

Infants under 2 years old can fly as lap children domestically at no extra charge. For international flights, airlines charge approximately 10% of the adult fare for a lap infant. Once your child turns 2, they require their own purchased seat at the child or adult fare. Our agents will clearly explain the exact cost structure for your infant on your specific route.

Purchasing a separate seat for your infant and bringing a FAA-approved car seat is the safest option, though not required. Many parents choose this for flights over 3-4 hours for both safety and practicality. Our agents at (866) 228-4952 can help you select the best seat position for a car seat and advise on specific airline car seat policies.

Bassinet seats (also called sky cribs or skycots) are available on many long-haul international flights and attach to the bulkhead wall. These are perfect for younger infants under about 6 months. They're free but must be reserved in advance — our agents can book these during your call.
