Budget airlines in the US operate on an 'unbundled' model — they charge the lowest possible base fare and then sell every additional element (baggage, seat selection, beverages, even printing your boarding pass) as optional add-ons. This model can produce genuine savings for travelers who understand the rules and fly light.

Spirit Airlines pioneered the ultra-low-cost model in the US and still operates with the lowest base fares of any major carrier. Their trademark yellow aircraft are instantly recognizable. Spirit charges for carry-on bags above personal item size, checked bags, seat selection, printing boarding passes at the airport, and drinks. For a traveler with just a personal item flying a direct route, Spirit can be genuinely cheap. With a checked bag, Spirit often costs more than mainstream alternatives.

Frontier Airlines has transformed into a Spirit-style ultra-low-cost carrier with a focus on environmental messaging. Their route network overlaps significantly with Spirit's and their fee structure is similarly aggressive. Frontier's Discount Den membership ($60/year) offers access to their cheapest fares and is worth considering for frequent Frontier flyers.

Many times we find that Southwest (with free bags) or other mainstream carriers beat budget airlines on total cost.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is Spirit Airlines actually cheaper than regular airlines?
Spirit has the lowest base fares, but add a checked bag ($45-79 each way), carry-on ($37-55), and seat selection ($13-40), and Spirit can cost more than American, Delta, or United. Call (866) 228-4952 and we'll calculate true all-in costs for your specific route.
What is Frontier's 'personal item' size allowance?
Frontier's free personal item must fit under the seat and measure no larger than 14 x 18 x 8 inches. A standard backpack usually qualifies. Carry-ons above that size cost $37-55 at booking, more at the gate. Always pre-pay for bags if needed.
Are budget airlines safe?
Yes. All US airlines, including budget carriers, are subject to the same FAA safety regulations, maintenance requirements, and pilot standards as major carriers. Budget means lower amenities, not lower safety.
