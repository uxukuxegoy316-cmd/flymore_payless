Houston is the fourth-largest city in the United States and one of the most culturally diverse — a fascinating mix of energy industry wealth, space exploration (NASA Johnson Space Center), world-class museums (Museum of Natural Science, Houston Museum of Fine Arts), and some of the best international dining in America thanks to its extraordinary immigrant communities.

Houston has two major airports: George Bush Intercontinental (IAH), United's second-largest hub, and William P. Hobby (HOU), Southwest's second-most important hub. IAH handles the lion's share of flights including all international service. Hobby is closer to downtown and handles primarily Southwest's domestic network.

United's massive IAH hub provides extensive domestic coverage and United's best international connectivity, including many Latin American routes. Southwest at Hobby offers competitive domestic pricing with free baggage. American, Delta, and numerous international carriers also serve IAH.

Houston's Gulf Coast proximity and subtropical climate make it genuinely hot and humid in summer (June-September). Spring (March-May) and fall (October-November) are ideal visiting seasons with comfortable temperatures and lower prices than major events periods. NASA Johnson Space Center is a must-visit attraction accessible from the city.

Should I fly into IAH or Hobby for Houston?
IAH has more airline options and handles international flights. Hobby (HOU) is closer to downtown and Southwest's stronghold. If Southwest serves your departure city, Hobby is convenient. Otherwise, IAH is likely your best option. Call (866) 228-4952 for a comparison for your specific route.
What is the cheapest time to fly to Houston?
January, February, and September offer the lowest prices. Summer is humid and hot but prices are moderate. Avoid major energy industry conference weeks when business travel spikes hotel and flight prices. Call (866) 228-4952 for current Houston fare comparisons.
What are the best things to do in Houston?
NASA Johnson Space Center, the Museum District (19 museums in walkable area), Buffalo Bayou Park, Galveston Island beach day trip, and Houston's extraordinary international dining scene are highlights. The city is genuinely underrated as a tourist destination.
