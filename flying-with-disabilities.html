The Air Carrier Access Act (ACAA) prohibits discrimination against passengers with disabilities and requires US airlines to provide specific accommodations. Knowing your rights and how to request assistance in advance makes a significant difference in your travel experience.

Wheelchair and mobility assistance is the most common accommodation requested by disabled travelers. Airlines must provide wheelchair assistance at the gate, jet bridge, and connecting areas at no charge. For passengers who cannot walk up stairs to board a jet bridge, airlines provide aisle chairs to board the aircraft. Pre-notification — ideally 48 hours in advance — ensures the assistance is ready when you need it.

For travelers requiring medical equipment including portable oxygen concentrators (POCs), CPAP machines, or other devices, advance coordination with the airline is essential. The FAA approves specific POC models for in-flight use — not all devices are permitted. Our agents at (866) 228-4952 help coordinate medical equipment approvals when booking.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is wheelchair assistance free on US airlines?
Yes, all US airlines must provide wheelchair assistance at no charge under the Air Carrier Access Act. This includes assistance at the check-in counter, security, gate, boarding, deplaning, and baggage claim. Request it when booking — call (866) 228-4952 and we'll arrange it for your journey.
Can I bring my CPAP machine on a plane?
Yes. CPAP machines are exempt from carry-on size restrictions on most airlines. Batteries for CPAP use must be approved lithium batteries. Some airlines provide distilled water for CPAP machines — ask when booking. Our agents at (866) 228-4952 will confirm the specific policy for your airline.
How early should travelers with disabilities arrive at the airport?
Arrive at least 30-60 minutes earlier than the standard recommendation to allow time for assistance coordination. Wheelchair assistance can sometimes involve waiting periods. Inform our agents at (866) 228-4952 about any mobility needs when booking so the airline is pre-notified.
