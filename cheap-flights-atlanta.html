Atlanta's Hartsfield-Jackson International Airport is the world's busiest airport by passenger count, and that extraordinary level of activity creates genuine competition and flight deal opportunities. Delta's massive hub operation means dozens of flights daily to virtually every US destination.

Delta dominates Atlanta with about 75% of all flights. This hub concentration means Delta's prices at ATL are often lower than at airports where they face more competition — counterintuitively, the airline's hub status creates rather than limits affordability on many routes. Southwest, American, and United also serve Atlanta with competitive pricing.

As a major connection hub, Atlanta serves travelers not just flying to the city but transiting through it. If you're flying Southeast to Northeast or cross-country, an Atlanta connection can sometimes be cheaper than a direct flight. Our agents at (866) 228-4952 always check Atlanta connections when building complex domestic itineraries.

The cheapest periods to visit Atlanta are January, February, and the quieter stretches of spring and fall. Major events like DragonCon (Labor Day weekend) and SEC Championship (December) drive prices up significantly for those specific weekends.

How far is Atlanta airport from the city?
